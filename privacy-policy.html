FireWatch Privacy Policy

Effective Date: [June 23, 2026]

1. Introduction

FireWatch ("the App") is developed and operated by Travis Brault ("I", "me", or "my"). This Privacy Policy explains how I collect, use, store, and protect your personal information when you use FireWatch on iOS or Android devices.

FireWatch is designed for firefighters and emergency responders to track operational readiness by combining wearable health data with shift and call data. I take your privacy seriously, especially given the sensitive nature of the health data involved.

By using FireWatch, you agree to the practices described in this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree, please do not use the App.

2. Information I Collect

2.1 Account Information

When you create an account, I collect:

  • Full name
  • Email address
  • Your age, which you provide during onboarding. Your age is used to estimate heart-rate zones (such as maximum heart rate). I do not collect your exact date of birth; for internal calculation your age is stored only as an approximate year of birth.
  • Authentication credentials (managed securely via Google Sign-In, Apple Sign-In, or email/password)
  • Station, unit/apparatus, and crew information you provide

2.2 Health and Fitness Data

With your explicit permission, FireWatch reads the following health and fitness data from Apple Health (iOS) or Google Health Connect (Android):

  • Heart rate
  • Resting heart rate
  • Heart rate variability (HRV)
  • Sleep data (duration and sleep stages)
  • Step count
  • Workout and exercise session data
  • Active energy / calories burned

This data is accessed read-only. FireWatch does not write data back to Apple Health or Google Health Connect.

2.3 Operational Data

FireWatch may store data you manually enter or that is generated by the App, including:

  • Fire call logs and shift reports
  • Recovery and readiness scores calculated by the App
  • Badges and achievements earned
  • Crew membership information
  • Shift schedule and notification preferences

2.4 Device and Usage Information

I may collect limited device information necessary to deliver push notifications, including your device push token. This token is used solely to send notifications to your device.

I also collect anonymous app-usage and crash information through a privacy-respecting analytics tool (PostHog) to understand how the App is used and to find and fix bugs. This information is limited to anonymous usage events (such as which screens are viewed and which features are used) and crash and error reports. It is not linked to your name, email, or account, and it never includes your health data. PostHog may generate an anonymous device or installation identifier for this purpose; this identifier is not tied to your identity. See Section 3 and Section 11 for more detail.

2.5 What Stays on Your Device vs. What Is Uploaded

FireWatch is designed to keep your raw health data on your device. This distinction is important:

  • Stays on your device: The raw, detailed health records read from Apple Health or Google Health Connect — including individual heart-rate samples, HRV readings, sleep sessions and stages, step records, workout records, and active-energy records — remain on your device. FireWatch uses these locally to calculate readiness, recovery, trends, and heart-rate zones.
  • Uploaded to Supabase: FireWatch uploads derived daily summary values — not your raw health streams — so the App can sync your readiness and challenge progress across sessions. These derived daily aggregates may include: sleep hours, sleep efficiency, average HRV (and the HRV metric type), resting heart rate, and step count. Operational and account data described above (calls, readiness scores, badges, profile, crew membership, notification preferences, push token) is also stored in Supabase to operate the service.

2.6 Information I Do Not Collect

FireWatch does not collect, store, or transmit your location. The App does not request location permissions and does not track your GPS position. I also do not collect advertising identifiers, and the App does not contain advertising.

3. How I Use Your Information

I use your information exclusively to provide and improve FireWatch. Specifically:

  • To calculate your operational readiness and recovery scores
  • To display your shift summaries, trends, and performance insights
  • To enable crew features, including crew boards and crew challenges, so members of your crew can view shared readiness and challenge information (see Section 6 for exactly what is shared)
  • To send push notifications including shift reports, badge alerts, weekly summaries, and burnout alerts
  • To authenticate your identity and maintain your account securely
  • To understand app usage and diagnose crashes through anonymous analytics, so I can improve the App's features and stability

I use privacy-respecting product analytics and crash reporting (PostHog) to understand how the App is used and to fix problems. This information is anonymous and is not linked to your identity, your account, or your health data. I do not sell your personal information, I do not use your data for advertising, and I do not use advertising trackers or track you across other apps or websites.

4. Health Data — Special Provisions

Health data is among the most sensitive personal information. I take additional precautions:

  • Health data is collected only with your explicit permission through your device's health platform (Apple Health or Google Health Connect)
  • You can revoke health data permissions at any time through your device Settings
  • Raw health data stays on your device; only derived daily summary values are uploaded (see Section 2.5)
  • Health data and derived values are used only to calculate readiness, recovery, and related insights within the App
  • Health data is never included in analytics. The anonymous analytics described in Sections 2.4 and 3 never contain your health data or derived health values.
  • Health data is never sold, never shared with advertisers, and never disclosed to third parties except as described in Section 6 (crew sharing and service providers) or as required by law
  • You can request deletion of your data at any time by contacting me

5. Data Storage and Security

The data that is uploaded (see Section 2.5) is stored using Supabase, a cloud database provider with industry-standard security practices including encryption at rest and in transit.

I use HTTPS for data in transit, and Supabase Row Level Security policies restrict access to your own records and to crew-scoped records (data visible only to members of the same crew where a crew feature requires it).

I implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect your personal information against unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. While I strive to protect your data, no method of transmission over the internet or electronic storage is 100% secure. I cannot guarantee absolute security.

6. Information Sharing

I do not sell or rent your personal information. I may share your information only in the following limited circumstances:

  • With crew members (profile and board): If you join a crew within the App, your name, avatar, station/unit, readiness score, lifetime call count, and earned badges are visible to other members of that crew.
  • With crew members (challenges): If you participate in crew challenges, members of that crew can see challenge leaderboard values that are derived from your health data, including your Recovery Score (a normalized value derived from your HRV), and your sleep and step values for the relevant challenge. Crew members do not see your raw Apple Health or Google Health Connect data — only these derived challenge values, and only for members of the same crew, not all FireWatch users.
  • With service providers: I use third-party services (including Supabase for database hosting, Expo for push notifications, and PostHog for anonymous analytics and crash reporting) that process data on my behalf. See Section 11.
  • For legal compliance: If required by applicable law, regulation, court order, or government request.
  • For safety: If I believe disclosure is necessary to protect the rights, property, or safety of users or others.

A note on profile photos

If you add a profile photo (avatar), please be aware that avatar images are stored in a publicly readable location, and the image's web address is based on your account identifier. This means a profile photo you upload could be accessed by someone who has the address, including outside the App, not only by your crew. Please do not upload a photo you would not want to be publicly accessible. You can change or remove your avatar at any time.

7. Your Rights

FireWatch is available to users in Canada, the United States, and other regions. Your rights depend on where you are located.

7.1 Canadian Users — PIPEDA

As a Canadian resident, you have rights under the Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA) and applicable provincial privacy laws, including:

  • The right to access the personal information I hold about you
  • The right to correct inaccurate or incomplete information
  • The right to withdraw consent for the collection or use of your personal information
  • The right to request deletion of your personal information
  • The right to know how your information is being used

To exercise any of these rights, please contact me at firewatchtravis@gmail.com. I will respond to your request within 30 days.

7.2 California Users — CCPA

If you are a California resident, you have additional rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), including:

  • The right to know what personal information I collect, use, and disclose about you
  • The right to request deletion of your personal information
  • The right to opt out of the sale of your personal information — Note: I do not sell your personal information
  • The right to non-discrimination for exercising your CCPA rights

To submit a CCPA request, contact me at firewatchtravis@gmail.com. I will respond within 45 days as required by California law.

7.3 All Other US Users

While there is currently no single comprehensive federal privacy law in the United States, I extend the same data access, correction, and deletion rights described above to all users regardless of location. Contact me at firewatchtravis@gmail.com to make any such request.

7.4 HIPAA Disclaimer

FireWatch is a personal wellness and operational readiness application. I am not a healthcare provider, health plan, or healthcare clearinghouse as defined under the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA). FireWatch is not a HIPAA-covered entity and the health data you share with the App is not protected health information (PHI) under HIPAA. The health data collected is used solely for personal fitness and readiness tracking purposes.

8. Data Retention

I retain your personal information for as long as your account is active or as needed to provide you with the App's services. If you delete your account, I will delete your personal information within 30 days, except where I am required to retain it by law.

Derived health values synced to Supabase are retained only for as long as necessary to provide readiness scores and insights. You may request deletion of this data at any time.

9. Account Deletion

You can request account deletion from inside the App. When deletion runs, FireWatch attempts to remove your stored avatar, push tokens, notification records, crew membership, badges, readiness scores, call records, profile records, and your authentication account, and clears app data stored locally on your device before signing you out. Related records are removed when your account deletion completes.

If a problem occurs during deletion, the App will show an error and provide contact information so any remaining data can be removed. You can also contact me directly at firewatchtravis@gmail.com for deletion assistance.

10. Children's Privacy

FireWatch is not intended for use by individuals under the age of 18. I do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 18. If I become aware that I have collected personal information from a child under 18, I will take steps to delete that information promptly.
