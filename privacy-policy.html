FireWatch Privacy Policy

Effective Date: June 27, 2026

1. Introduction

FireWatch ("the App") is developed and operated by Travis Brault ("I", "me", or "my"). This Privacy Policy explains how I collect, use, store, and protect your personal information when you use FireWatch on iOS or Android devices.

FireWatch is designed for firefighters and emergency responders to track operational readiness by combining wearable health data with shift and call data. I take your privacy seriously, especially given the sensitive nature of the health data involved.

By using FireWatch, you agree to the practices described in this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree, please do not use the App.

2. Information I Collect

2.1 Account Information

When you create an account, I collect:

  • Full name
  • Email address
  • Your age, which you provide during onboarding. Your age is used to estimate heart-rate zones (such as maximum heart rate). I do not collect your exact date of birth; for internal calculation your age is stored only as an approximate year of birth.
  • Authentication credentials (managed securely via Google Sign-In, Apple Sign-In, or email/password)
  • Station, unit/apparatus, and crew information you provide

2.2 Health and Fitness Data

With your explicit permission, FireWatch reads the following health and fitness data from Apple Health (iOS) or Google Health Connect (Android):

  • Heart rate
  • Resting heart rate
  • Heart rate variability (HRV)
  • Sleep data (duration and sleep stages)
  • Step count
  • Workout and exercise session data
  • Active energy / calories burned

This data is accessed read-only. FireWatch does not write data back to Apple Health or Google Health Connect.

2.3 Operational Data

FireWatch may store data you manually enter or that is generated by the App, including:

  • Fire call logs and shift reports
  • Recovery and readiness scores calculated by the App
  • Badges and achievements earned
  • Crew membership information
  • Shift schedule and notification preferences

2.4 Device, Notification, and Usage Information

I may collect limited device information necessary to deliver push notifications, including your device push token. This token is used solely to send notifications to your device.

I also collect limited app-usage information through PostHog, a product analytics provider, to understand how the App is used and where the experience can be improved. This includes screen views and selected feature events, such as onboarding completion, health connection success, joining or creating a crew, opening a shift report, viewing a paywall, starting a purchase, completing a purchase, or restoring a subscription.

PostHog analytics events may be associated with an internal FireWatch account identifier after you sign in, and with a generated device or installation identifier before sign-in. I do not send your name, email address, raw health data, derived health values, call details, shift report contents, crew names, crew join codes, or free-text content to PostHog. FireWatch does not use PostHog for advertising, ad targeting, or tracking you across other apps or websites.

FireWatch has PostHog session replay, touch autocapture, screen autocapture, app lifecycle autocapture, and automatic GeoIP resolution disabled. This means PostHog is not configured to record your screen, capture taps, capture text input, automatically record every navigation event, or infer your approximate location from analytics events. See Section 3 and Section 11 for more detail.

2.5 What Stays on Your Device vs. What Is Uploaded

FireWatch is designed to keep your raw health data on your device. This distinction is important:

  • Stays on your device: The raw, detailed health records read from Apple Health or Google Health Connect — including individual heart-rate samples, HRV readings, sleep sessions and stages, step records, workout records, and active-energy records — remain on your device. FireWatch uses these locally to calculate readiness, recovery, trends, and heart-rate zones.
  • Uploaded to Supabase: FireWatch uploads derived daily summary values — not your raw health streams — so the App can sync your readiness and challenge progress across sessions. These derived daily aggregates may include: sleep hours, sleep efficiency, average HRV (and the HRV metric type), resting heart rate, and step count. Operational and account data described above (calls, readiness scores, badges, profile, crew membership, notification preferences, push token) is also stored in Supabase to operate the service.
